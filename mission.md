# PoliTechs.uk — Mission Statement

## The Problem

Politics and technology are reshaping each other at unprecedented speed — yet they operate in silos.

Policymakers and politicians often lack the technical literacy to legislate effectively on AI, data, digital infrastructure, and emerging technologies. At the same time, technologists — those building the tools that shape our lives — frequently lack civic engagement, political awareness, or meaningful routes into public discourse.

The result is predictable: legislation that lags years behind reality, technology that erodes democratic norms, and a society that is left behind — whether through algorithmic discrimination, surveillance capitalism, or the concentration of digital power in the hands of a few.

## Our Method

**PoliTechs.uk** exists to bridge this gap.

We bring together politicians, civil servants, technologists, researchers, journalists, and citizens who work at or care about the intersection of politics and technology. We do this through:

- **Events** — panel discussions, workshops, hackathons, and networking sessions
- **Conversation** — creating space for honest, cross-disciplinary debate on the key issues
- **Collaboration** — connecting people who can build, legislate, and advocate together

We focus on the issues that matter most:

- AI governance and regulation
- Digital rights and surveillance
- Open data and government transparency
- Civic technology and democratic participation
- Tech's role in public services (health, education, welfare)

## Our Goal

A society where technology serves the many, not the few.

We want Britain to lead the way in **responsible, human-centred innovation** — where democratic values shape the digital world, not the other way around. Where Parliament understands what it is legislating. Where technologists feel the weight of civic responsibility. And where citizens have the tools and knowledge to participate meaningfully in decisions about their digital future.

## We Believe

- Democracy and technology are inseparable — let's treat them that way.
- Better conversations between politics and tech lead to better outcomes for society.
- Change requires people: connected, informed, and motivated to act.
